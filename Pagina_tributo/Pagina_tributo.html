Alan Turing

The father of modern computer science and artificial intelligence

Here's a timeline of his life

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1931 - He entered the University of Cambridge to study mathematics.
  • 1934 - He was elected to a fellowship at King’s College in recognition of his research in probability theory.
  • 1936 - Have his seminal paper “On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem [Decision Problem]” recommended for publication by the American mathematical logician Alonzo Church which he introduces the Turing Machine, a theoretical model of a computer.
  • 1938 - He obtained his PhD in mathematical logic from Princeton University.
  • 1939 - Returns to the UK and joins the team working to break German codes during World War II, playing a crucial role in deciphering the Enigma code.
  • 1945 - World War II ends, and he continues to work on computing and artificial intelligence projects.
  • 1950 - He publishes the paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," where he proposes the "Turing Test" to evaluate a machine's intelligence.
  • 1952 - Turing is convicted of "gross indecency" due to his homosexuality, facing legal penalties and hormone therapy.
  • 1954 - Alan Turing passes away in Wilmslow, Cheshire, UK, under circumstances that suggest a possible suicide. He was only 41 years old.
  • 2013 - The UK government grants a posthumous royal pardon to Alan Turing, acknowledging the injustice of his criminal conviction for being gay.
